## Version 2: Standalone Post (under 600 words)

---

A claim is making the rounds that IQ is genetically tied to race. Before you pick a side, try three questions.

---

**Question 1: What is actually established?**

Some things are not in dispute. Standardized IQ tests show average score differences between self-identified racial groups in the US. IQ scores predict academic and occupational outcomes. Within a given population, IQ is substantially heritable in adults.

Those are real findings.

What is not established: that between-group score differences are primarily genetic. No specific genetic variants explaining the gap have been identified. The APA convened a dedicated task force in 1996 — a broadly constituted panel, not a partisan one. Their conclusion: "The cause of that differential is not known."

That is where the evidence currently sits.

---

**Question 2: Who benefits from the framing?**

This is where it gets instructive.

The claim that racial IQ gaps are genetic — and that this is being suppressed — did not emerge spontaneously. It has a documented supply chain: a funding network (the Pioneer Fund, founded 1937 with explicit eugenics goals), a parallel publication ecosystem that mimics academic form without standard peer review, think-tank infrastructure that benefits politically from the conclusion (if inequality is genetic, welfare and affirmative action are futile), and a contemporary Substack layer where deplatforming events directly increase subscriber revenue.

The "suppression" framing does specific rhetorical work: it makes any institutional rebuttal appear as further evidence of persecution. A claim that cannot be falsified is not a scientific claim.

---

**Question 3: What do we share?**

More than the argument suggests.

Both sides agree the score gap is real. Both agree IQ has substantial within-group heritability. Both agree that understanding the causes of educational inequality matters enormously for policy.

The actual disagreement is narrow and precise: whether the between-group gap is primarily genetic or primarily environmental. And the most powerful evidence on that question points clearly in the environmental direction.

The Flynn Effect: IQ scores rose roughly 3 points per decade across the entire 20th century, in every tested population worldwide. Genetics cannot change on that timescale. This is not disputed — it is the finding that forced every serious researcher to acknowledge massive environmental plasticity in the IQ phenotype.

The gap narrowing: the Black-white IQ gap in the US narrowed by an estimated 4 to 7 points between 1970 and 2000 (Dickens and Flynn, 2006). That is within a single generation. Genetic composition of populations does not shift measurably in 30 years.

The core logical error: within-group heritability cannot be extrapolated to between-group differences. Arthur Jensen himself acknowledged this in his 1969 paper — then made the inference anyway. Every subsequent hereditarian popularization has repeated the inference and omitted the acknowledgment. This is not a subtle point. It is a textbook error in population genetics.

---

**What we can actually do**

If the educational gap is substantially environmental — driven by lead exposure disparities, school funding gaps, poverty, and documented historical disadvantage — then it is addressable. Environmental causes have environmental solutions. This is not a concession to ideology. It is the conclusion the evidence supports, and it reopens the policy space that biological determinism forecloses.

Both the people calling Cofnas a racist and the people sharing his Substack want honest science. The question is whether the claim can survive the three questions applied consistently.
